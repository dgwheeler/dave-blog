Once upon a time, there was a bright and bubbly girl named Maya who loved school. She always had her backpack neatly organized, her pencils sharpened, and her homework completed on time. But one sunny Monday morning, Maya woke up late and rushed out the door—forgetting to check her backpack.
When she got to Mrs. Johnson's classroom, she sat down and realized with a sinking heart that her math homework was missing! She had left it on her kitchen table, and now it felt like a weight on her shoulders. Her stomach twisted into knots as she watched Mrs. Johnson collect the other students' papers.
Maya's face turned as red as a fire truck. The teacher was about to collect the assignments. "Maya, is everything okay?" Mrs. Johnson asked gently. Maya shook her head no, tears welling up in her eyes.
Mrs. Johnson smiled kindly. "Don't worry, Maya. Everyone forgets things sometimes. You can bring it tomorrow morning, and I'll make sure you get full credit."
That afternoon, Maya rushed home to get the homework, but by then it was too late to bring it to school. Her mom found her crying on the kitchen floor, the missing homework spread out on the table. "Oh no, Maya! I forgot it at home!" she sobbed.
But Mrs. Johnson's kindness saved the day. When Maya handed in her homework the next morning, Mrs. Johnson gave her a special sticker and said, "This is for trying your best, even when you make mistakes."
From then on, Maya always did a final check of her backpack before leaving home. And she learned that even when you make mistakes, there's always room for forgiveness and a second chance.
